⚡ What is DVFS?

Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling lets the CPU operate at different power levels. By reducing frequency, voltage also drops, cutting power consumption dramatically.

P = C × V² × f

Lowering frequency from 2400 MHz to 600 MHz reduces power by ~87%. The scheduler picks the minimum frequency needed to meet each process's deadline.

📅 What is EDF?

Earliest Deadline First is a scheduling policy where the process with the nearest deadline always runs first. It is provably optimal for real-time systems — if a feasible schedule exists, EDF will find it.

Combined with DVFS, EDF assigns each process just enough CPU frequency to meet its deadline — no more, no less.

😴 CPU Sleep States (C-States)

When the CPU is idle between processes, it enters power-saving sleep states. Deeper sleep = more energy saved, but slightly longer wake-up time.

StatePowerWake TimeSavings
C1 Halt0.5 W~1 µs30%
C2 Stop0.2 W~10 µs50%
C6 Deep0.05 W~200 µs90%

🎯 Project Objective

  • Develop a CPU scheduling algorithm that minimizes energy consumption
  • Maintain performance and deadline guarantees for real-time tasks
  • Target mobile and embedded systems with limited battery life
  • Combine EDF scheduling with DVFS for optimal power management
  • Implement CPU sleep state (C-State) management during idle periods

📊 Real-World Applications

  • Android OS uses Energy Aware Scheduling (EAS) in Linux kernel
  • Apple Silicon uses efficiency + performance cores (same concept)
  • ARM big.LITTLE architecture assigns tasks by energy need
  • IoT devices (ESP32, STM32) run months on a single battery
  • Medical wearables: pacemakers, glucose monitors
  • Autonomous vehicles — real-time + energy critical
📈 Performance Metrics Explained
Energy (Joules)
Primary metric. Total electrical energy consumed during all process execution and idle periods.
Turnaround Time
Time from process arrival to completion. Measures overall system responsiveness and performance.
Deadline Miss Rate
Percentage of real-time tasks that exceeded their deadline. Lower is better — 0% is ideal for critical systems.
